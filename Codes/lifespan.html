Wolves are remarkable creatures, and their lifespan can vary significantly depending on their environment. In the wild, wolves typically live for 6 to 8 years, though some may reach 10 years or more under favorable conditions. However, life in the wild is fraught with challenges such as predation, starvation, injuries, diseases, and human-related threats like hunting and habitat destruction, which often limit their longevity. In contrast, wolves in captivity enjoy a much longer lifespan, ranging from 12 to 15 years, due to the absence of natural predators, regular feeding, and access to veterinary care. This stark difference highlights the impact of environmental factors on the survival of these majestic animals.
